  • The Seventh Hanwha Solar Forest, Incheon, Korea | English

    //v.kr.kollus.com/ZxZarxV2
    The Seventh Hanwha Solar Forest,
    Incheon, Korea     Over 100 Hanwha employees and volunteers from the general public came out on a chilly October morning in the outskirts of Incheon, Korea to help plant the seventh Hanwha Solar Forest. The location for the seventh Hanwha Solar Forest was specifically chosen because it lies directly along the path of atmospheric dust that have annually made their way across Korea from deserts overseas.
  • The Eighth Hanwha Solar Forest,
    Samcheok, Korea     On April 20th 2021, Hanwha Group celebrated the completion of its 8th Solar Forest. 15,000 saplings have been planted to match the number of virtual trees raised by campaign participants on the Solar Forest mobile app. Black locust saplings were specifically chosen to create the carbon-neutral forest to maximize carbon capture as they absorb vast amounts of greenhouse gases. The 8th Solar Forest is Hanwha’s contribution to the Korea Forest Service’s ’Forest Strategy for Carbon Neutrality 2050’ to reduce carbon emissions by 34 million tons by planting 3 billion trees over the next 30 years.
  • The Ninth Hanwha Solar Forest,
    Hongcheon, Korea     In celebration of Earth Day, on April 20, 2022, Hanwha Group unveiled its 9th Solar Forest. The 12,000 pine trees and larches planted in the “Hongcheon Carbon Absorbing Forest” are expected to absorb up to 102 tons of carbon dioxide while purifying 2,000 tons of oxygen and 0.53 tons of fine dust from the air every year. The Solar Forest app, which had exceeded 160,000 downloads at the time of the launch, was able to amass 16,356 virtual tree donations in total, surpassing the original goal of 12,000 trees.

Clean Up Vietnam

In Vietnam, Hanwha is closely collaborating with government agencies and NGOs to address the serious environmental issues the country faces. From using solar-powered boats to clean up the Mekong River to educating and spreading awareness on responsible consumption, Hanwha’s actions are making a definite and positive impact on the country.

  • Hanwha Launches Campaign to Help Clean Vietnam’s Mekong River To commemorate World Environment Day on June 5, Hanwha is launching the “Clean Up Mekong” campaign in Vinh Long Province in collaboration with the Vietnam Environment Administration and the Global Green Growth Institute (GGGI) of Vietnam. The campaign will combat the pollutants floating in the Mekong River. Central to the campaign’s launch was the unveiling ceremony for solar-powered boats, donated by Hanwha, that will be used to clean the river.

Happy Sunshine

Happy Sunshine Campaign is a CSR activity that Hanwha is actively pursuing in order to prevent global warming and climate change. The program uses environmentally friendly solar energy to create a warmer society and bring more energy to life. We have installed solar power generation systems at 320 centers, producing a total of about 2,187 kW of solar energy.

  • Hanwha Q CELLS, Hanwha China Bring Light to Schools in Poverty-Stricken Regions Hanwha Q CELLS and Hanwha China has made a second
    donation of solar power generation equipment to
    a school in an impoverished region in China, as part
    of a campaign with the China Youth Development
    Foundation (CYDF). A 30 kW rooftop solar system was
    installed at the Xiegouxiang Hope School, located in
    Datong, Qinghai Province, China. Hanwha Happy
    Sunshine Campaign aims to make a positive impact
    on creating a prosperous future for our nations as well as
    for all of humanity through solar power, a clean
    renewable energy, and Hanwha Group will continue
    to work closely with local communities as a partner
    for the economic and social development of China.
  • Providing Free Solar Power for Welfare Facilities Hanwha has implemented the “Happy Sunshine” campaign for welfare facilities across the nation every year since 2011. All the facilities, which offer social welfare services, such as social welfare centers and community child centers can apply to the campaign. 118 systems have been installed in South Korea
    since 2011 and over the next ten years, Hanwha plans to donate more than 500 additional ones. The group receives their application online, selects beneficiaries together with World Vision and Community Chest Of Korea, and supports the installment of solar power faculties.
  • Hanwha 'Happy Sunshine' Once Again Brings Hope to the Children of China Hanwha Group and Hanwha SolarOne today officially announced it will donate a 30 kW solar PV system that will provide continuous power support to Xiegou Town Center Primary School in Datong County, Qinghai Province.
  • Happy Sunshine Goes to Japan, Fulfilling a Promise It Made a Year Ago Hanwha also helped Japan hit by the Great East Japan Earthquake in March of 2011, by donating 1 billion won worth of solar power systems, noodles in a cup, and construction materials and promised that it would provide solar power systems for schools and kindergartens in the areas where electricity grid broke down such as Aomori, Iwate, Miyagi, and Fukushima.
